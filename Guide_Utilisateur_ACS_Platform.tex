\section{Introduction}

The ACS Pricing Decision Platform is an interactive analysis and simulation tool for sulfuric acid (ACS) pricing, designed for contract negotiations with OCP.

The tool comprises \textbf{three modules}:

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{>{\bfseries\color{primary}}l X}
\toprule
Module & Description \\
\midrule
Monte Carlo Simulation & Future price projections with confidence intervals and spike scenarios. \\[6pt]
Formula Lab \& Decision & Backtest a formula against the real market, then test the formula on Monte Carlo scenarios. \\[6pt]
Contract Impact Analysis & Revenue analysis by volumes, OCP scenarios (70--100\,\%), inflation. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\begin{infobox}[Data Source]
Historical prices and projections come from \textbf{S\&P Global annual data}. Market reference: \textbf{ACS CFR North Africa}. Variable index: \textbf{ACS FOB NW Europe}.
\end{infobox}

\section{Monte Carlo Simulation}

This module generates price projections through \textbf{Monte Carlo simulation}: from an S\&P trend and historical volatility, hundreds of possible trajectories are simulated, with the option to add price spikes.

\subsection{Parameters}

All parameters are located in the \textbf{left sidebar}.

\begin{table}[H]
\centering\small
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{>{\bfseries}l X}
\toprule
Parameter & Description \\
\midrule
Product & Variable to simulate (ACS CFR North Africa, ACS NW EU, S ME, DAP, etc.) \\[3pt]
Horizon & Start year and end year of the projection \\[3pt]
Override S\&P & Modify S\&P outlook values per year (check ``Override S\&P values'') \\[3pt]
Petcoke \& Clinker & Optional override of petcoke and clinker outlooks (for F6) \\[3pt]
Volatility & ``Use Historical'' computes volatility from data. Multiply to adjust. \\[3pt]
Spikes (Frequency) & Number of spikes per year (price shocks) \\[3pt]
Spikes (Intensity) & Spike amplitude in \% \\[3pt]
Spikes (Persistence) & Spike duration in months \\[3pt]
Decay Type & Exponential or linear spike decay \\[3pt]
MC Paths & Number of simulated trajectories (100 to 2\,000) \\[3pt]
Smoothing & Brownian motion smoothing (0.3 to 0.9) \\[3pt]
Random Seed & Seed for result reproducibility \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{02_mc_metrics.png}
  \caption{Monte Carlo simulation metrics and parameters.}
\end{figure}

\subsection{Results}

After clicking \textbf{Run Simulation}:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em]
  \item \textbf{5 metrics} at the top --- Average S\&P Outlook, Simulated Mean, 95th pctl max, 5th pctl min, Volatility used.
  \item \textbf{Projection chart} --- Blue band (95\,\% interval), red dashed line (S\&P trend), blue line (individual scenario selected via slider).
  \item \textbf{3 histograms} --- Price distribution for 3 years (end $-$ 2, end $-$ 1, end).
\end{itemize}

\section{Formula Lab \& Decision}

This module has \textbf{two functions}: (1) test a formula against the market history (\textit{backtest}), and (2) apply this formula to Monte Carlo scenarios to assess future risk.

\subsection{The Six Formulas}

The user selects \textbf{one formula at a time} from the dropdown menu. Each formula has its own adjustable parameters.

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{c l X}
\toprule
ID & Name & Principle \\
\midrule
F1 & Sulfur Indexing & Direct sulfur indexation (ME/NA) + production cost \\[3pt]
F2 & Smooth Sulfur & Like F1, but with smoothed sulfur average \\[3pt]
F3 & Last Month ACS & Previous month ACS price (regional weighting) \\[3pt]
F4 & S \& DAP Variation & Sulfur + DAP variation relative to references \\[3pt]
F5 & Smooth S \& DAP & Like F4, with smoothed inputs \\[3pt]
F6 & Full Cost Stack & Sulfur + DAP + petcoke + clinker (4 components) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\subsection{Formula Parameters}

Each formula exposes its coefficients (a, b, c\ldots), regional weights, and reference prices. A \textbf{Floor} and a \textbf{Cap} can be enabled or disabled via a toggle.

\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{12_formula_performance.png}
  \caption{Formula selection, equation, adjustable parameters, and Floor/Cap toggle.}
\end{figure}

\subsection{Backtest --- Historical}

The backtest compares the selected formula to the real market over history (2018--2025 by default, adjustable via slider).

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em]
  \item \textbf{Formula vs Market chart} --- Two curves: market price (red) and formula price (blue). Floor/Cap lines if enabled.
  \item \textbf{P\&L chart} --- Green bars (formula below market = buyer advantage) or red bars (formula above = disadvantage). Quarterly or annual view.
\end{itemize}

\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{13_contract_override.png}
  \caption{Backtest: market price (red) vs formula price (blue) over history.}
\end{figure}

\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
    \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{14_project_revenue.png}
  \caption{Historical P\&L: green/red bars per quarter with average.}
\end{figure}

\subsection{Performance Summary}

Five cards summarize the backtest:

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{>{\bfseries}l X}
\toprule
Card & Description \\
\midrule
Avg P\&L & Average formula vs market spread (\$/t) \\[3pt]
Win Rate & \% of periods where the formula is below the market \\[3pt]
Best Period & Best spread in favor of the buyer \\[3pt]
Worst Period & Worst spread (formula above market) \\[3pt]
Annual Impact & Annualized financial impact in \$M (750\,KT) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}


\subsection{Decision --- Test on Monte Carlo Scenarios}

\begin{warningbox}[Prerequisite]
You must first run a Monte Carlo simulation in \textbf{Tab 1} to generate future scenarios. Then return here to apply the selected formula.
\end{warningbox}

This section applies the selected formula to a Monte Carlo scenario to evaluate its future behavior.

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em]
  \item \textbf{Annual Volume} --- Annual volume (default 750\,KT) for the financial calculation.
  \item \textbf{MC Scenario} --- Slider to choose a scenario from those generated.
  \item \textbf{Formula vs MC chart} --- Formula applied to the future scenario (simulated market price vs formula price).
  \item \textbf{Future P\&L} --- Green/red bars over the simulated period.
  \item \textbf{3 cards} --- Market Cost, Formula Cost, Savings (\$M/yr).
\end{itemize}

\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
    \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{15_project_prices.png}

  \caption{Formula applied to a future Monte Carlo scenario.}
\end{figure}

\subsection{Risk Analysis --- All MC Scenarios}

The tool automatically computes formula savings across \textbf{all MC scenarios} (up to 200) and displays:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em]
  \item \textbf{Expected Savings} --- Average savings across all scenarios.
  \item \textbf{Best Case (P95)} --- Savings in the most favorable scenario.
  \item \textbf{Worst Case (P5)} --- Savings in the least favorable scenario.
  \item \textbf{Prob. of Savings} --- \% of scenarios showing savings.
  \item \textbf{Histogram} --- Savings distribution across all scenarios.
\end{itemize}

\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{18_scenario_summary.png}
  \caption{Risk Analysis: Expected/Best/Worst/Probability cards + savings histogram.}
\end{figure}

\begin{infobox}[Decision Support]
If ``Prob. of Savings'' is $> 50$\,\% and the ``Worst Case'' remains acceptable, the formula offers good protection. Compare multiple formulas by changing the selection in the dropdown menu.
\end{infobox}

\section{Contract Impact Analysis}

This is the main module for contract analysis. It models project revenues and evaluates the OCP impact over the contract duration.

\subsection{Parameters}

Parameters are displayed \textbf{at the top of the tab} (not in the sidebar), in three columns.

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{>{\bfseries}l c X}
\toprule
Parameter & Default & Description \\
\midrule
Total Production Volume & 750 KT & Annual production capacity \\[4pt]
Fixed Price & 110 \$/t & Contractual fixed price (base year) \\[4pt]
OCP Purchase Volume & 70--100\,\% & Share purchased by OCP. 70\,\% = all at fixed \\[4pt]
Inflation Rate & 2\,\% & Annual inflation rate on the fixed price \\[4pt]
Coefficient A & 1.0 & FOB NW Europe multiplier \\[4pt]
Premium B & 0 \$/t & Premium/discount added to the variable price \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

Below the parameters, three \textbf{formula boxes} display in real time the fixed price, variable price, and volume allocation.

\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{05_formula_params.png}
  \caption{Contract Impact parameters: 3 columns + formula boxes.}
\end{figure}

\begin{warningbox}[Volume Logic]
OCP is required to buy at least \textbf{70\,\% of production} at the \textbf{fixed price}.
The slider ranges from 70\,\% to 100\,\%. Any volume above 70\,\% is purchased at the \textbf{variable price} (formula $A \times \text{FOB}_{\text{NWE}} + B$).

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.2em]
  \item At 70\,\%: OCP buys 525\,KT, all at the fixed price.
  \item At 85\,\%: OCP buys 637\,KT = 525\,KT fixed + 112\,KT variable.
  \item At 100\,\%: OCP buys 750\,KT = 525\,KT fixed + 225\,KT variable.
\end{itemize}
\end{warningbox}

\subsection{Formulas}

\begin{formulabox}
\textbf{Fixed Price (with inflation):}
\[
P_{\text{fixed}}(t) = P_{\text{base}} \times (1 + i)^{t - t_0}
\]
where $i$ = annual inflation rate, $t_0$ = first year of the filter.

\textbf{Variable Price:}
\[
P_{\text{var}} = A \times \text{FOB}_{\text{NW Europe}} + B
\]
\end{formulabox}

\subsection{Year Range \& Market Override}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em]
  \item \textbf{Year Range} --- Slider to restrict the analysis period (e.g., 2022--2035).
  \item \textbf{Override market price projections} --- Check to enter custom market prices per year. All charts recalculate automatically.
\end{itemize}

\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
    \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{06_formula_backtest.png}
  \caption{Market price override: fields per year to modify ACS CFR North Africa prices.}
\end{figure}

\subsection{Perspective Selector}

A radio button toggles between two views: \textbf{Project Perspective} (seller) and \textbf{OCP Perspective} (buyer).

\subsection{Project Perspective}

Seller's point of view: how much revenue the project generates.

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em]
  \item \textbf{5 metrics} --- Avg Revenue, Weighted Avg Price, Market Ref Price, Negotiated Price, vs Market (\%).
  \item \textbf{Business Plan Revenue} --- Stacked bars: fixed revenue (blue) + variable revenue (yellow) per year.
  \item \textbf{Volume Split} --- Stacked bar showing the fixed/variable allocation in KT.
  \item \textbf{Price Comparison} --- 5 curves: Market Ref (black), Weighted Avg (yellow), Fixed Price with inflation (dashed blue), Negotiated Price (dashed red), FOB NW Europe (purple).
  \item \textbf{Sensitivity} --- Table comparing different volume splits (50/50, 60/40, \ldots, 100/0) and their impact on average revenue.
\end{itemize}

\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
    \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{07_formula_pnl.png}
  \caption{Business Plan Revenue: stacked bars for fixed (blue) and variable (yellow) revenue per year.}
\end{figure}


\subsection{OCP Perspective}

Buyer's point of view: how much OCP saves compared to the market.

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em]
  \item \textbf{4 metrics} --- OCP Avg Cost, Market Cost, Avg Value Gain, Avg Price Paid.
  \item \textbf{OCP Value Gain} --- Bars per year. Blue = fixed portion gain, yellow = variable portion gain. Negative bars extend below zero (barmode ``relative'').
  \item \textbf{OCP Price Paid vs Market} --- 3 predefined scenarios (70\,\% fixed, 85\,\%, 100\,\%) + market.
  \item \textbf{Cumulative Value Gain} --- Cumulative gain over time for the 3 scenarios.
  \item \textbf{Breakeven Analysis} --- Market (black) vs blended contract (yellow) vs fixed with inflation (dashed blue). Shaded area = savings zone.
  \item \textbf{Scenario Comparison Summary} --- Summary table: volume, cost, gain for each scenario.
\end{itemize}


\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{10_contract_params.png}
  \caption{Breakeven Analysis: market vs contract with savings zone.}
\end{figure}

\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{18_scenario_summary.png}
  \caption{Scenario Comparison Summary: summary table of the 3 OCP scenarios.}
\end{figure}

\begin{warningbox}[Key Point]
When the market is high, the fixed-price contract benefits OCP (positive gain). When the market drops below the fixed price, the gain becomes negative. Inflation increases the fixed price each year, gradually reducing the advantage.
\end{warningbox}

\section{General Features}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=6pt]
  \item \textbf{Automatic recalculation} --- All charts update as soon as a parameter changes.
  \item \textbf{Time filtering} --- ``Year Range'' or ``History from'' sliders to restrict the period.
  \item \textbf{Chart export} --- Hover over a chart and click the camera icon to save as PNG.
  \item \textbf{Data export} --- Open the ``Detailed Data'' expanders to copy-paste into Excel.
  \item \textbf{Interactive legends} --- Click a series in the legend to hide/show it.
\end{itemize}
